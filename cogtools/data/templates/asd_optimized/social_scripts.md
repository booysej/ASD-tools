# Social Scripts for Common Situations

## 🏢 Workplace Scripts

### Small Talk Responses

**When someone asks "How was your weekend?"**
- "It was good, thanks. I [specific activity]. How was yours?"
- "Pretty relaxing. I caught up on [specific task/hobby]."
- "Busy but productive. What about you?"

**When passing colleagues in hallway**
- Smile and nod
- "Morning!" / "Hey!"
- "Hi [Name]" (if you know them)

**Ending conversations gracefully**
- "I need to get back to [specific task]. Good talking with you!"
- "I should run - have a deadline coming up. Catch you later!"
- "Thanks for the chat. I'll let you get back to it."

### Meeting Participation

**Asking for clarification**
- "Could you elaborate on [specific point]?"
- "I want to make sure I understand - are you saying [restate]?"
- "What would that look like in practice?"

**Disagreeing professionally**
- "I see your point. Have we considered [alternative]?"
- "That's interesting. My experience has been [your view]."
- "I have a different perspective on this..."

**Buying thinking time**
- "That's a good question. Let me think for a moment."
- "I'd like to consider that and get back to you."
- "Can I take that as an action item and follow up?"

### Email Communication

**Starting emails**
- **Formal**: "I hope this email finds you well."
- **Neutral**: "I'm reaching out regarding..."
- **Follow-up**: "Following up on our conversation about..."

**Ending emails**
- **Formal**: "Best regards," / "Sincerely,"
- **Friendly**: "Thanks!" / "Best,"
- **Action needed**: "Looking forward to your response."

## 👥 Personal/Social Scripts

### Declining invitations

**When you need alone time**
- "Thanks for thinking of me! I can't make it this time."
- "I appreciate the invite, but I have other commitments."
- "That sounds fun, but I need a quiet evening. Maybe next time?"

**When sensory issues are a factor**
- "Thanks! [Venue/Activity] isn't really my thing, but I appreciate you including me."
- "I'm not great with [crowds/loud music/etc], but thanks for asking!"

### Setting boundaries

**At work**
- "I focus best with minimal interruptions. Could we schedule a time to discuss?"
- "I'm most productive when I can work on one thing at a time."
- "I need to process this information. Can I get back to you tomorrow?"

**With friends/family**
- "I care about you, but I need some space right now."
- "I'm feeling overwhelmed. Can we talk about this later?"
- "I need to recharge. Let's reconnect in [timeframe]."

### Phone calls

**Answering professionally**
- "Hello, this is [Your Name]."
- "[Your Name] speaking."
- "Hi, you've reached [Your Name]."

**Ending calls**
- "I should let you go. Thanks for calling!"
- "I need to run, but this was helpful."
- "Let's follow up on this via email."

## 🆘 Difficult Situations

### When you don't understand sarcasm/jokes
- Smile and give a small laugh
- "I'm not sure I follow..."
- "Could you explain? I think I missed something."

### When overwhelmed in social settings
- "Excuse me, I need to use the restroom." (Take a break)
- "I'm going to step outside for some fresh air."
- "I need to make a phone call. Be right back."

### When you've made a social mistake
- "I apologize if that came across wrong."
- "Let me rephrase that..."
- "Thank you for your patience with me."

## 💭 Internal Scripts (Self-Talk)

### Before social events
- "I can leave whenever I need to."
- "I'll focus on one conversation at a time."
- "It's okay to take breaks."

### During overwhelming moments
- "This feeling will pass."
- "I can excuse myself if needed."
- "One breath at a time."

### After social interactions
- "I did my best with the energy I had."
- "It's okay if that wasn't perfect."
- "I'm learning and improving."

## 📝 Customization Tips

1. **Adapt to your style** - These are templates, not rules
2. **Practice key phrases** - Rehearse the ones you'll use most
3. **Keep it simple** - Shorter responses are often better
4. **Be authentic** - Choose scripts that feel natural to you
5. **Document what works** - Add your own successful scripts here
