# 🧠 Psychology-Based Features in CogTools

## Overview

CogTools has been enhanced with comprehensive psychology-based features specifically designed for individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and their families. These features are grounded in evidence-based research and therapeutic practices including:

- **Sensory Processing Theory** (Ayres)
- **Theory of Mind** research (Baron-Cohen)
- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Autism** (CBT-A)
- **PEERS® Social Skills Intervention**
- **Dunn's Sensory Profile** assessment
- **Social Thinking® Methodology**
- **Interoception Training** principles

---

## 🎭 Sensory Processing Toolkit

### **Sensory Profile Assessment**
Based on Winnie Dunn's Sensory Profile research, this tool assesses sensory processing patterns across five main systems:

#### **Assessment Categories:**
- **Auditory Processing**: Response to sounds, noise sensitivity, auditory seeking
- **Visual Processing**: Light sensitivity, visual stimulation preferences
- **Tactile Processing**: Touch sensitivity, texture preferences
- **Vestibular Processing**: Movement and balance responses
- **Proprioceptive Processing**: Body position awareness, pressure needs

#### **Features:**
- 5-point rating scale from "Very Sensitive" to "Very Seeking"
- Personalized sensory strategies based on profile
- Evidence-based recommendations for each sensory system
- Adaptive strategies for sensory avoiders and seekers

### **Sensory Diet Planner**
Creates structured sensory activity schedules based on individual needs:

#### **Time-Based Activities:**
- **Morning Routine**: Heavy work, deep pressure, organizing activities
- **Midday Support**: Movement breaks, fidget tools, visual breaks
- **Evening Calming**: Proprioceptive input, breathing exercises

#### **Customization Options:**
- Personal activity additions
- System-specific targeting (tactile, vestibular, etc.)
- Completion tracking for effectiveness monitoring

### **Interoception Training**
Develops body awareness and emotional recognition skills:

#### **Core Components:**
- **Guided Body Scans**: 2-15 minute exercises with instructions
- **Emotion-Body Mapping**: Connecting feelings to physical sensations
- **Daily Check-ins**: Rating hunger, thirst, energy, muscle tension
- **Progress Tracking**: Building interoceptive awareness over time

#### **Research Basis:**
Based on Dr. Kelly Mahler's interoception research and its connection to emotional regulation in ASD.

## 🤝 Social Skills Trainer

### **Social Story Creator**
Implements Carol Gray's Social Stories™ methodology:

#### **Story Structure:**
- **Descriptive Sentences**: Objective facts about situations
- **Perspective Sentences**: Others' thoughts and feelings
- **Directive Sentences**: Specific behavioral guidance
- **Control Sentences**: Self-regulation strategies

#### **Pre-Built Templates:**
- Restaurant visits
- Meeting new people
- Birthday parties
- Doctor appointments
- School presentations
- Dealing with changes and frustration

#### **Features:**
- Customizable templates for any situation
- Visual formatting with emojis for ASD learners
- Save and review functionality
- Evidence-based structure for maximum effectiveness

### **Theory of Mind Trainer**
Progressive exercises based on Baron-Cohen's research:

#### **Six Training Levels:**
1. **Basic Emotions**: Simple emotion recognition
2. **Wants and Preferences**: Understanding others' desires
3. **Beliefs and Knowledge**: What others know vs. what's true
4. **False Beliefs**: Classic Sally-Anne type tasks
5. **Complex Emotions**: Embarrassment, pride, mixed feelings
6. **Social Situations**: Complex social reasoning

#### **Features:**
- Interactive scenarios with multiple choice responses
- Immediate feedback with explanations
- Progressive difficulty levels
- Real-world application examples

### **Conversation Practice**
Structured social interaction training:

#### **Practice Scenarios:**
- Starting conversations with peers
- Asking teachers for help
- Joining group conversations
- Making plans with friends
- Disagreeing politely
- Ending conversations gracefully

#### **Learning Features:**
- Multiple response options with feedback
- Explanation of why responses work or don't work
- Built-in conversation rules and tips
- Safe practice environment

## 🧠 CBT Toolkit for ASD

### **Thought Records**
Adapted CBT thought challenging for ASD thinking patterns:

#### **Structured Process:**
1. **Situation**: Factual description of events
2. **Emotions**: Multiple emotion selection with intensity ratings
3. **Thoughts**: Automatic thought identification
4. **Evidence**: For and against the thought
5. **Alternative Thoughts**: More balanced perspectives
6. **Re-evaluation**: Changed belief in original thought

#### **ASD Adaptations:**
- Clear separation of facts vs. interpretations
- Visual emotion intensity sliders
- Concrete evidence-gathering prompts
- Step-by-step guidance through the process

### **Behavioral Experiments**
Safe testing of worried thoughts and beliefs:

#### **Experiment Design:**
- **Prediction**: What do you think will happen?
- **Confidence Rating**: How sure are you?
- **Safety Planning**: What if you feel overwhelmed?
- **Results**: What actually happened?
- **Learning**: What did this teach you?

#### **Features:**
- Structured experiment planning
- Safety considerations built-in
- Outcome vs. prediction comparison
- Learning consolidation

### **Coping Strategies Toolbox**
Comprehensive ASD-specific coping techniques:

#### **Strategy Categories:**
- **Immediate Calming**: 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, box breathing, deep pressure
- **Thinking Strategies**: Thought stopping, fact vs. opinion, probability checking
- **Social Strategies**: Script preparation, safe person identification, exit planning
- **Sensory Strategies**: Environmental modification, sensory breaks, input regulation
- **Planning Strategies**: Visual schedules, transition warnings, backup plans

#### **Features:**
- Tried-it tracking for effective strategies
- Personal strategy additions
- Situation-specific recommendations
- Evidence-based techniques

### **Mood and Anxiety Tracker**
Simple daily emotional monitoring:

#### **Tracking Elements:**
- Overall mood (1-10 scale)
- Anxiety level (1-10 scale)
- Contributing factors (sleep, social, sensory, routine, etc.)
- Daily notes
- Pattern recognition over time

#### **Benefits:**
- Identifies mood triggers
- Tracks intervention effectiveness
- Provides data for therapy sessions
- Builds emotional awareness

## 🚀 Implementation Guidelines

### **Getting Started**
1. **Begin with Assessment**: Start with sensory profile and mood tracking
2. **Identify Priority Areas**: Focus on most challenging daily situations
3. **Gradual Introduction**: Add new tools slowly to avoid overwhelm
4. **Consistent Use**: Daily practice builds skills and habits
5. **Celebrate Progress**: Acknowledge small wins and improvements

### **Best Practices**
- **Individualize**: Adapt tools to personal preferences and needs
- **Collaborate**: Involve family, therapists, and educators
- **Monitor**: Track what works and adjust accordingly
- **Patience**: Allow time for skills to develop and generalize
- **Support**: Use tools as supplements to, not replacements for, professional support
